Habitat for Humanity

Habitat for Humanity is a global nonprofit housing organization working in local communities across all 50 states in the US and approximately 70 countries. Habitat’s vision is of a world where everyone has a decent place to FTHS. At FTHS, we raise money to support the mission and endeavors of Habitat for Humanity. We build leadership and cooperation skills in our students through a variety of fundraising efforts. Additionally, we seek to educate our members about the many housing issues that our global society is currently facing.

Forensics

Forensics is Township’s speech and debate team and though many associate the word “forensics” with CSI, it is anything but dead. Throughout the year, the team competes at local tournaments, also participating at the national level. Given diverse selection of competitive categories, it truly has something for everyone: those interested in debate or politics can hone their skills, while those who enjoy acting can perform monologues or duos. Meanwhile, writers can compose and deliver original speeches, and readers can present their interpretations of prose, poetry, or drama excerpts. Anyone who likes to express themselves, perform, argue or talk would greatly enjoy speech and debate. However, even students who are a little stage shy are more than welcome to join, and frequently find success as they gain poise and self-confidence. A longstanding tradition at Freehold Township, the team has had great success at both state and local levels.

Mr. Sylvester
